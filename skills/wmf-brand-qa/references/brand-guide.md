# WMF Brand Guide - AI Working Version

Source: `260319_WMF_Brand_Book_2026_screen.pdf`, March 2026. Page references below point to the source PDF.

## 1. Brand core

WMF is a premium German brand rooted in metal craftsmanship, iconic design, professional expertise, and innovation. It creates unique kitchenware and culinary experiences for upscale restaurants, hotels, and homes. The brand idea is: **Taste Matters.** The reason to believe is: **Inspired by professionals. Designed for home.** (pp. 5, 7-12, 16)

### Positioning pillars

- German tradition and quality: founded in 1853 in Geislingen, Germany.
- Iconic design: authentic to metal, considered in detail, holistic, and genuinely iconic.
- Professional expertise: decades of experience with professional tools, especially coffee machines, brought into the home.
- Metal crafting: confidence in the beauty and performance of metal; Cromargan® is a core proprietary technology.

Do not reduce WMF to “German heritage” alone. The brand combines heritage with innovation, design, and professional expertise. (pp. 8, 16, 18)

### Audience

The primary prospect is the passionate home chef: someone who loves cooking, experiments with techniques, demands superior quality and tools, and appreciates cultivated design. Communication should remain aspirational and welcoming to a wider audience. (p. 15)

### Personality

German, sophisticated, passionate, innovative. WMF speaks to consumers as equals and has a clear point of view. It should entertain, educate, and showcase what is state of the art. (pp. 16, 25)

## 2. Voice and copy

The voice is premium, clear, concise, direct, emotionally engaging, brand-honouring, and innovative. (p. 25)

### Do

- Use easy-to-understand language.
- Make direct, relevant statements.
- Show both functional and emotional benefits.
- Express substance and passion for cooking.
- Use fresh, unique, inspirational angles.
- Highlight meaningful product features, heritage, design, and cooking expertise.

### Avoid

- Complex sentences and elaborate wording.
- Empty marketing language, hollow phrases, and irrelevant claims.
- Excessive superlatives.
- A generic, mass-market, rustic, or overly commercial tone.

### Useful brand language

- “Taste Matters” is the central brand belief and should support brand-level communication.
- “Inspired by professionals. Designed for home.” is the core reason-to-believe line.
- “Anything but ordinary” expresses the emotional benefit for the passionate home chef.
- “Cromargan®” must retain the registered-trademark symbol when used as the technology name.

Avoid inventing claims about performance, manufacturing origin, or professional endorsements. Product claims require product-specific substantiation.

## 3. Visual identity

### Logo

- The WMF logo is the foremost visual identity element.
- Prefer the white logo on a dark background; use black on a light background only when appropriate.
- Keep safe space: one “M” above and below, and at least one full logo height at the sides.
- Centre the logo. For portrait formats, keep at least one logo height from the sides; for landscape formats, keep three logo heights from the sides; keep one “M” height from the top edge.
- Do not cover important image elements such as products or faces.

Never distort proportions, use an outline, apply a shadow, apply a gradient fill, or use unapproved colours. (pp. 19-23)

### Typography

The brand typeface is WMF Rotis. Use Regular, Regular Italic, and Bold. Do not use the Light or Medium families. Licensing and installation come from WMF Content Studio. (p. 27)

For the copy system shown in the source: use generous tracking for key claims, optical kerning, and approximately 125% line spacing for copy blocks. Follow the relevant production template for exact sizing. (p. 28)

## 4. Image and art direction

### Overall visual language

WMF should feel elevated, minimalistic, modern, confident, design-oriented, and cosmopolitan. It should not feel overly commercial, rustic, playful, retro, mainstream, or country. (pp. 43-46)

### Image principles

- Use clean, premium, product-first compositions.
- Prefer less-is-more layouts with refined, uncluttered visuals.
- Use strong light against deep shadow and high contrast.
- Keep imagery overall bright, never excessively dark.
- Use cool-toned shadows with warm or beige highlights.
- Show professional chefs and taste makers in aspirational, modern environments.
- Make products the hero and avoid distracting props or clutter.

Lighting should have strong contrast. Cromargan® should be visibly and convincingly represented: glossy products need shaped highlights and hard edges; matt products should retain matt highlights and shadows; stainless-steel brushing should remain visible. Do not make Cromargan® look uniformly black or unnaturally matt. (pp. 45, 48-50)

### Imagery types

- Campaign visuals: communicate the campaign idea and claim; use in campaign context.
- Brand lifestyle visuals: express the “perfect moment with WMF” and “Taste Matters” idea in brand contexts.
- Atmospheric stills: heroically stage a product; use for product fascination.
- Real stories: show authentic situations and the people behind the story.
- Recipe visuals: make ordinary food feel extraordinary while remaining recognisable and inviting.

(pp. 51-53)

### Settings, props, and food

Use modern, clean, premium architectural environments; professional kitchens, home-chef kitchens, and taste-maker homes. Prefer stainless steel, marble, stone, and concrete, with a considered balance of warm and cool tones. Props should be clean, linear, understated, purposeful, and never compete with the product. Food should be professional yet approachable, recognisable, authentic, and inviting; avoid unnecessary luxury ingredients such as lobster. (pp. 55-58)

### Casting and styling

Cast diverse, relaxed, confident people, broadly 30-50, who move naturally on camera. Avoid a visibly mass-commercial or overly staged look. Home-chef styling is modern and design-conscious but practical for cooking; professional chefs may wear aprons or chef jackets. Avoid light clothing, knitwear, oversized sleeves, and impractical accessories. (pp. 59-61)

## 5. Channel and content guidance

- Packaging should feel high quality, iconic, minimalistic but impactful, strongly branded, and consistent across product groups.
- Product detail pages should be clear, consistent, and premium, using storytelling to communicate brand value and product benefits.
- Social media can be more playful, authentic, approachable, and focused on real stories while maintaining WMF’s visual language.
- Video supers are uppercase, may communicate a product feature or reason to believe, and should be centred, left-, or right-aligned according to the format.

(pp. 34-41)

## 6. Heritage and legal-use cautions

The German Heritage Label is for brand communication and must appear with the WMF logo; it should not be used in pure product communication. “Made in Germany” is reserved for products actually manufactured in Germany and may be used in specified product communications. Verify current legal and market approval before applying either label. (p. 29)

Use registered marks accurately, especially Cromargan® and Cromargan Protect®. Verify claims, endorsements, image rights, and manufacturing-origin statements before publication.

## 7. Asset requirements

For source assets, the book specifies: images at minimum 300 ppi and at least 2500 px on the short side; single-layer images as TIFF; layered images as PSD; Adobe RGB (1998), 8-bit; default aspect ratios 2:3 or 3:2 unless briefed otherwise. Video minimum is Full HD 1920x1080, MP4. (p. 63)

Maintain copyright and usage metadata. For AI-generated content, set the author metadata to identify the creator and that it is AI generated. Do not infer usage rights from the brand book. (p. 64)
